SCHEME INTERPRETER

November 2016, Cal Hacks

This was my final project for CS 61A. My partner and I developed an interpreter in Python for a subset Scheme language that includes primitive expressions, user-defined functions, dynamic scoping, and supports tail recursion.

UNITE

November 2016, Cal Hacks

INSPIRATION

My team and I were inspired by the recent outbreak of protests throughout the nation. We saw the importance of staying safe while voicing opinions and decided to create a communication platform that allowed individuals to gain an immersive experience during protests, while staying safe.

Our iOS app allows individuals to find a protest near them, check in, and create a protest. For each protest, there is a safety bar that uses user-inputed data to calculate how safe the protest is.

CONTRIBUTIONS

After creating a mid-level prototype of the app using Figma, I focused on the backend. I utilised Swift to implement things like going from a address to latitude/longitude points, adding pins to a map, and adding data.

WHAT I LEARNED

This was my first hackathon, and my first time using Swift. Learning the basics of Swift was definitely a challenging task, but I enjoyed working with my team members to implement new things. Because it was our first time making an app, we weren't able to fully perfect or implement all of our ideas, but I am happy with what we were able to accomplish!
